Right place to explore and learn interseting facts about 'e'

  • The number e is known as "Euler's number", named after the mathematician Leonard Euler.
  • Maths has many important constants such as i(iota),(pi), but e is equally important but if not more important than many other constants.

    • Heros behind "e"

    In the 17th century, John Napier, a Scottish mathematician started finding shortcuts to find exponents. He came up with some algorithms in which he unknowingly used e.

    In 1683 Jacob Bernoulli, a Swiss mathematician discovered e while solving problems related to compound interest.

    In 1731 another Swiss mathematician Leonard Euler proved that e is irrational. He did this by expanding e in the form of an infinite series of factorials.

1.Compound Interest

Let initial investment be 5000 rupees
Assume that a bank pays 100% interest per year
In one year your 5000 rupees will turn into 10000 rupees.
Now imagine that the bank pays back twice a year in a 6 month interval(i.e. 50 % and 50%)
Hence halfway through the year you will have 7500 rupees and on reinvesting your 7500 rupees will become 11250 rupees.

Compounding Formula=(1 + r/n)n Where r=annual interest rate as decimal n=number of periods the year is split into
As we keep dividing the year into smaller and smaller periods the value of the compounding formula approaches e and this is how it was first discovered by Jacob Bernoulli.
This occurs due to the similarity between
Compounding Formula=(1 + r/n)n
e(n tending to infinity) = (1 + 1/n)n

2. Half Life

It is the time taken by a substance to lose half of its activity
The decay happens exponentially is a time dependent decline
Half life can be used to estimate the age of ancient artifacts,fossils,etc by a process called carbon dating
T1/2 = ln 2 / λ
Where λ is decay constant

4.Probability Theory

Suppose a gambler is playing a game of roulette
If he plays the game n times the probability that he wins the game k times is given by nCk *(1/n)k*(1-1/n)n-k
the probability of the gambler losing all games i.e. winning 0 games is (1-1/n)n
The limit of which is 1/e as n tends to infinity
